\title{Height Bounds for Height-Balanced Trees}
\author{Manuel Eberl}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This article takes a fresh look at the class of binary trees known as \emph{height-balanced trees}, where for each node, the height difference between the left and right subtree is bounded by some fixed integer $d > 0$. An interesting question from an algorithmic perspective is how bad the imbalance of such trees can be in the worst case. Luccio and Pagli~\cite{luccio} showed that the worst-case size of a tree of height $h$ is roughly $B_d\cdot C_d^h$ for large $h$ for some specific real numbers $B_d, C_d > 1$.

This formalisation contains:
\begin{itemize}
\item A simpler proof that the worst-case size is at least $C_d ^ h$ for all $h$
\item An explicit closed-form expression for the worst-case size
\item A closed-form expression for $B_d$ in terms of $C_d$ and vice versa
\item Explicit bounds for $C_d$ in terms of $d$
\item Asymptotic expansions for $C_d$ and $B_d$ as $d\to\infty$
\end{itemize}
\end{abstract}
