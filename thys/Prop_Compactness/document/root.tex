\begin{abstract}
This theory formalises the compactness theorem for propositional logic
based on the model existence theorem approach. It also presents
applications of compactness theorem to formalize combinatorial
theorems over countable structures:  the De Bruijn-Erdös Graph
coloring theorem for countable graphs, König Lemma, and set- and
graph-theoretical versions of Hall's Theorem for countable families of
sets and graphs.  The theory has been updated following constructive
recommendations gently provided by reviewers. 
\end{abstract}
