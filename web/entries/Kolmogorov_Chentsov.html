Abstract

Continuous-time stochastic processes often carry the condition of having almost-surely continuous paths. If some process \(X\) satisfies certain bounds on its expectation, then the Kolmogorov-Chentsov theorem lets us construct a modification of \(X\), i.e. a process \(X'\) such that \(\forall t. X_t = X'_t\) almost surely, that has HÃ¶lder continuous paths.

In this work, we mechanise the Kolmogorov-Chentsov theorem. To get there, we develop a theory of stochastic processes, together with HÃ¶lder continuity, convergence in measure, and arbitrary intervals of dyadic rationals.

With this, we pave the way towards a construction of Brownian motion. The work is based on the exposition in Achim Klenke's probability theory text.
