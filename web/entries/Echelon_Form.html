Abstract

We formalize an algorithm to compute the Echelon Form of a matrix. We have proved its existence over B√©zout domains and made it executable over Euclidean domains, such as the integer ring and the univariate polynomials over a field. This allows us to compute determinants, inverses and characteristic polynomials of matrices. The work is based on the HOL-Multivariate Analysis library, and on both the Gauss-Jordan and Cayley-Hamilton AFP entries. As a by-product, some algebraic structures have been implemented (principal ideal domains, B√©zout domains...). The algorithm has been refined to immutable arrays and code can be generated to functional languages as well.
